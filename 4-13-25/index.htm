Squaredle

Squaredle is a daily word search game.
Read on, or find more questions & answers in the Squaredle FAQs.
Subscribe now for premium puzzles & features.

How do I play?

Find words by connecting letters up, down, left, right and diagonally. Squares fade out once you've found all the words using them. Can you clear the whole board?
Tap the word count to see which words you've found and how long the remaining words are.
When your progress bar reaches the first star, letters on the board will show a number indicating how many words start with that square. Reach the star after that for another new trick!
Squaredle, like Scrabble, doesn't accept capitalized or hyphenated words.

When does the next puzzle come out?

New puzzles come out each day at 3am Pacific.
Monday puzzles are the easiest; Fridays are the hardest. Saturday puzzles have fun twists, and Sundays have tons of words.
Each day also brings a new 3x3 Express puzzle.
Stuck? Try rotating the board with the rotate icon.

Can I use a physical keyboard?

Yes! Type out the letters, then press <Enter> to submit the word. Use <Backspace> to delete a letter. The <Space bar> toggles between squares that have the same letter, if you're a visual thinker.

What's with the score?

Longer words are worth more points, bringing you closer to unlocking the next perk. Most daily puzzles have a word that is at least 8 letters long.
The globe icon represents the average of everyone's score today. Try to beat it!

What's a bonus word?

Obscure, archaic, and (some) slang words are "bonus" words — they're not part of your score, but they show up as "+1" next to the word count. The leaderboard shows who's found the most bonus words. More about bonus words

Words

Squaredle has licensed the official NASPA word list, NWL2023, to determine which words are valid. We then separate these into required and bonus words based on how familiar American English speakers are with these words.

    Bonus word
    

  

  

    

      You've found a bonus word!
    

    

      (In the tutorial puzzle, all words except the tutorial words are treated
      as "bonus" words.)
    

    

      Bonus words make the base game easier by excluding obscure or questionable
      words. Finding them won't progress your score, but they show up on the
      leaderboards.
    

    

      Bonus words include:
      
    
        
  • Archaic, obscure or domain-specific words
          
      
            
    • eyne, arty, graviton
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
        
  • Non-American English words
          
      
            
    • colour, tyre, brae
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
        
  • Some slang
          
      
            
    • holla, nosh
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
      

    

    

      There are usually about 2x more bonus words than required words. Bonus
      words ensure that puzzles remain accessible to all, while recognizing
      players' diverse vocabularies.
